Learn the house. Earn trust in delivery. Leave reusable capacity.

A hypothesis-led entry plan to be adapted with Crowe Studio leadership and the assigned engagement team.

The first 90 days should prove three things: I can work directly in the solution, own a complete bounded workstream, and make the practice stronger through what the engagement teaches.

Entry principles

Delivery before commentary

Join real work quickly: discovery, requirements, prompts/context, configuration, tests, stakeholder preparation, and issue resolution.

Learn standards through use

Understand Crowe’s methods, platforms, estimation, governance, and global delivery interfaces by applying them under experienced review.

Make decisions observable

Use a compact record for outcome, workflow, authority, evidence, integration, ownership, and the next decision.

Harvest reusable learning

Convert delivery lessons into a connector note, evaluation asset, workshop mechanism, control pattern, or estimation insight.

Measures for the plan

Measure categoryWhat I would seek to observe
Client movementA decision made, workflow changed, working slice used, risk retired, or clear stop/reframe decision.
Delivery qualityRequirements traceability, test evidence, exception handling, human authority, and clean handoffs.
AdoptionReal user participation, repeat use, feedback incorporated, operating owner and support path.
Practice leverageA reusable asset, documented lesson, improved estimate, or clearer qualification signal.
TrustIncreasing autonomy granted by engagement leadership and confidence in executive/client communication.

Days 61–90 · Compound the engagement

  • Take responsibility for the workstream’s delivery rhythm, stakeholder alignment, and executive status communication.
  • Close at least one full loop from observed failure or user friction to tested improvement and documented result.
  • Harvest one reusable practice asset: evaluation set/template, connector pattern, action-envelope control, workshop exercise, estimation heuristic, or adoption measure.
  • Support opportunity qualification or proposal shaping with grounded technical feasibility, delivery risk, evidence standard, and first-value logic.
  • Mentor through paired work: share the mechanism, invite critique, and document what another consultant can reuse.
